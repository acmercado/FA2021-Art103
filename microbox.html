"microbox"

title:

microbox

date:

10/07/2021

medium:

breadboard, xiao microcontroller, speaker, amplifier, 2 leds, toggle switch, momentary button, rotary potentiometer, and sliding potentiometer (2 220 ohm & 2 10k ohm resistors).

editing the code

I tried putting different songs in the "Peter Gun" code but their tempos are different, so I will revist this when I have time to change the tempo/ how many notes should be played overall. Although it's very slight, the difference in the melodies upon using the momentary button and potentiometers could be heard.

description/process:

Setting up all of the components on the breadboard wasn't difficult, considering the circuits we studied and the circuit we had to do for our breadboard quiz were quite similar. However, once I put them all in my box, it was difficult to figure out which wire was which even though I color-coded them so that I wouldn't have this problem. I also over-estimated how long my wires should be, so I had to move the wires around in order for the box to actually close. Unfortunately, doing this made my components "sensitive" which just tells me that my connections need improvement and/or I should consider using a heat gun instead of the soldering iron to shrink the shrink wraps to prevent from any connections moving around. This was especially evident in my sliding pot, which would have a delayed reaction of fading the red LED. Interestingly, my speaker also started "breaking" because of how loud the songs were with the amp (especially when I tried to test other songs and change them to faster tempos), but it still worked nonetheless. (Without the amp, the microbox would be too quiet to hear).

. . .

Editing the original "Peter Gun" code to play different songs, was a challenge. I knew what the code programmed the microbox to do, (not only through what was written but also through the demonstration of the box), but the melodies I chose were too fast at its lowest setting and would be even faster at its highest setting (using the rotary potentiometer). I know the problem lies within the code that I replaced, so I just need to go over it again and fix any mistakes/find how to make the lowest setting be at the songs' normal tempos.
